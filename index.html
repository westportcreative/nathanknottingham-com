About Nathan

Fourth-generation Oregonian. First-generation Texan.

I started in mortgage in 2007, which means my first year in the business was everyone else's worst. That timing taught me everything: the loan officers who survived were the ones who knew their craft and told the truth.

Since then I've originated, run training and operations for a national mortgage school, co-founded Vetted VA with Christopher Griffith, and built MLO Force into an NMLS-approved education provider. I write for HousingWire, speak at industry conferences, and serve as president of my local chamber of commerce.

Off the clock I'm a husband to Billie Julene, dad to Titus and Trinity, a church bass player, and a firm believer that a well-tied bow tie fixes most problems.
