The federal minimum is only half the story.

Here is the trap. A loan officer completes twenty hours of pre-licensure education, eight hours of continuing education a year, and assumes they are covered everywhere. Then they get licensed in a new state and discover that state wants additional hours on its own laws. The renewal stalls, the pipeline freezes, and nobody warned them.

The SAFE Act sets a national floor for every mortgage loan originator: 20 hours of pre-licensure education (PE) before you are licensed, and at least 8 hours of continuing education (CE) every year to renew. That federal 8 hours is fixed by rule: three hours of federal law, two hours of ethics, two hours of non-traditional mortgage lending, and one hour of elective content.

On top of that floor, many states add their own state-specific hours. Some add nothing. Some, like Nevada, push the pre-licensure total all the way to thirty. This page separates the two clearly for all fifty states plus the District of Columbia, so you can see your real number instead of guessing.

Questions

NMLS education, answered

The rule is simple: if you can't explain your requirement, don't wait until renewal week to figure it out.

How many hours of NMLS continuing education do I need each year?

+
The federal SAFE Act requires at least 8 hours of NMLS-approved continuing education every year to renew a mortgage loan originator license. That 8 hours breaks down into 3 hours of federal law, 2 hours of ethics, 2 hours of non-traditional mortgage lending, and 1 hour of elective. Some states require additional state-specific hours on top of the federal 8, so your true total depends on where you are licensed. Use the map above to see your state.

How many hours of pre-licensure education are required to become an MLO?

+
The federal minimum is 20 hours of NMLS-approved pre-licensure education. Several states require more. Nevada requires 30 total hours, Utah requires 25, and North Carolina, Ohio, and West Virginia each require 24. Your state's exact requirement is shown when you click its tile.

Do all states require state-specific NMLS education?

+
No. Many states only require the federal minimum of 20 pre-licensure hours and 8 continuing education hours. Others add state-specific hours covering that state's own mortgage laws. This page shows both the federal baseline and any additional state hours for every jurisdiction.

Can I use AI or shortcuts to complete NMLS courses?

+
No. Under NMLS Rules of Conduct, AI assistance is not permitted for course completion, identity verification is required, and seat time is strictly enforced. Violations can result in loss of course credit, fines, or license action. Complete your education honestly through an NMLS-approved provider.

Where do I complete my NMLS continuing education?

+

When is the NMLS continuing education deadline?

+
The NMLS renewal period typically runs from November 1 to December 31 each year, and continuing education must be completed before you can renew. Individual state agencies can set their own deadlines and rules, so confirm your state's specific dates in the NMLS Resource Center and finish your CE early to avoid a lapse.
